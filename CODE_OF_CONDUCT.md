# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers pledge to make participation in the
`Canimation` community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age,
body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender
identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status,
nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity
and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming,
diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Taking responsibility, apologizing, and learning from mistakes
- Focusing on what is best for the community as a whole

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, threats, or discriminatory language
- Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Spam, repeated hostile review behavior, or intentionally disruptive conduct

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing standards of
acceptable behavior and may take appropriate and fair corrective action in response
to behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces, including issues, pull
requests, code review, documentation discussions, release discussions, and any other
community channels associated with this repository.

## Reporting

If you need to report a conduct issue, do not start a public argument in the issue or
pull request thread. Report the incident privately through the maintainers' GitHub
contact channels or the security/reporting path documented in `SECURITY.md` when a
private disclosure path is more appropriate.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers may respond with one or more of the following actions depending on the
severity and pattern of behavior:

- correction
- warning
- temporary restriction from project interaction
- permanent removal from project spaces
